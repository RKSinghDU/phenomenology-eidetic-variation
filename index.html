About this activity: Based on Husserl's method of free imaginative variation. Students generate varied instances of an emotion, then systematically remove candidate features to determine which are essential (invariant across all instances) and which are accidental (variable without destroying the phenomenon). The goal is an eidetic definition — not a personal account, but the invariant structure of a type of experience.
